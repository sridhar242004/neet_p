States of Matter: Gases and Liquids

In chemistry, gases are characterized by their ability to expand to fill any container, high compressibility, and lack of fixed shape or volume. Liquids, while also lacking a fixed shape, have a definite volume and exhibit properties like surface tension and viscosity, enabling them to flow and conform to their container's shape. These states play vital roles in various scientific and industrial applications.
