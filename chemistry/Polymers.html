Polymers

Polymers are large molecules composed of repeating structural units, or monomers, connected by covalent chemical bonds. They play a crucial role in chemistry and everyday life, serving as the basis for materials ranging from plastics and fibers to biological macromolecules like DNA and proteins. The diversity of polymers allows for tailored properties such as strength, flexibility, and biodegradability, making them essential in various industries including healthcare, electronics, and manufacturing.
