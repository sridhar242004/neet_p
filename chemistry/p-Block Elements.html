p-Block Elements

p-Block elements in chemistry refer to those elements in the periodic table where the last electron enters the p orbital. These elements span groups 13 to 18 and include a diverse range of properties and characteristics. They exhibit a wide variety of oxidation states and bonding patterns, making them crucial in the formation of numerous compounds essential to both biological systems and industrial applications. p-Block elements play significant roles in environmental chemistry, materials science, and pharmaceuticals, highlighting their importance across various scientific disciplines.
